transition goals followup

December 16, 2016   

#transitiongoals followup

Some general thoughts: it’s been fascinating/fun/interesting to see how my priorities changed as I arrived in California.

“Personal growth” things

Meet five (5) people in a one-on-one setting

Meeting people socially did happen, but not in this fashion. This goal came out of two things: not having substantial friendships after my previous summer internships happened, and a desire to branch out and get to meet new people by confidently inviting them out to places. The first did happen, in a different fashion: I met other interns my age with whom I think I will have lasting friendships. Having an extended co-op (May through December), plus having the ability to go out on long road trips to amazing places in California (like the Marin Headlands and Mammoth Lakes) heavily contributed to this.

However, I still want to start/get a lot better/practice meeting people and having lunch one-on-one. I can think of a couple of candidates in the Bay Area I can start with.

Visit five (5) iconic places in nature

Here’s one I accomplished. The final list of twelve, after eight months:

  • Marin Headlands
  • Land’s End
  • Castle Rock State Park
  • Mission Peak
  • Half Moon Bay
  • Big Sur
  • Lake Tahoe (twice - once in summer, once for winter skiing)
  • Mammoth Lakes
  • Mojave Desert
  • Death Valley
  • Grand Canyon
  • Zion National Park

I still have a lot that I want to tackle, however - being in California has only made my list of places longer.

  • Pinnacles National Park
  • Sequoia National Park
  • Joshua Tree National Park
  • Salton Sea
  • Mt. Shasta
  • The rest of the Sierras (likely some sort of CA-395 highway drive)

Take an amazing photo every week

Looking back on the photos that I’ve taken from this co-op, I likely got very close to this, despite only using my iPhone (didn’t even bring my dSLR). Especially near the end of this co-op, I was essentially there - my Thanksgiving trip had multiple amazing photos each morning and afternoon. You can see my Instagram for proof. Earlier on, however, I did not have as many amazing/adventurous weekends, which ended up being my primary opportunities for amazing photos.

In conclusion: I got close to this goal through finding amazing settings for photographs. I should take the weeks in which I don’t have these opportunities to try to be more artsy. I’m also seriously considering buying myself a Fuji X100T, a great small portable camera that I could throw in my backpack and take anywhere.

Read 24 books

I got nowhere close with this. I read a total of one and a half books. I first started Between the World and Me by Ta-Nehisi Coates in June, which I then ended up restarting and finishing on my flights back home in December. In September, I also read Flight Club by Chuck Palahniuk, which a coworker insisted I borrow from him.

This goal is something that I’m beginning to perceive as even more desirable. A lot of people I’ve started admiring for their thoughts are clearly well-read, and not just in Internet thinkpieces. I’m just not sure how to gain time to achieve it.

Document what I created and consumed for every day

I (with about 80-90% accuracy) have tracked my podcast diet over my time in California. I didn’t track the TV or Internet that I consumed as well, largely due to little TV consumption and an incredible amount of Internet consumption. I could/should start TV consumption tracking. I’m not sure how to track Internet consumption. I downloaded RescueTime a couple of weeks ago, which feels rather intrusive but could have useful results.

I plan to make a post about my podcast diet and the podcasts that I recommend soon.

Become physically fit

I’ve gained 5-10 pounds since I left Michigan. Being in an office and buying a car means I’ve been decently sedentary during the week, despite going out and hiking on weekends. Buying an Apple Watch helped me track and understand my sedentary-ness, and I have put time in my schedule during next semester for working out.

Physical activity every day

This didn’t happen. I went to the gym only a couple of times, although I tried to get out and hike every weekend.

Gym three times a week

I went about three times in eight months.

Run a 5k without stopping

I did go for a few runs during August, but never into a good routine (or beyond 2.25 miles). I think if I set a specific 5k as my goal and set a training schedule, I could hold myself to it. This seems like a good goal for after I graduate, and have a consistent life schedule.

Do technical things

Apply for grad school

I wrote a draft statement of purpose and talked to two professors about recommendation letters, but this ultimately didn’t happen. I didn’t think I could put forward a competitive application for the high-caliber Ph.D. and master’s programs I was looking for. I also received a full-time offer in the Bay Area, that I decided to take.

Return offer

I received a return offer, and accepted it.

Volunteer with Chezy Champs

This goal was intended to be a small step towards a larger goal. Rejoining the FIRST Robotics community seemed like a good, constructive use of my time outside of work. Attending Chezy Champs felt like a good first step to rejoining the community, and finding a good role within it.

However, two things have conspired against this goal.

Firstly, I don’t think rejoining the FIRST Robotics community is the best use of my time outside of work. I think I need to focus on fulfilling a lot of the goals that I missed (physical activity and exercise, reading books), exploring California, and trying to do constructive things in new areas and fields.

Secondly, Chezy Champs was the same weekend as I was able to attend the Southern California taping of the Grand Tour as a member of the audience, one of my favorite TV shows for years. This link talks about the taping the day before for the new opening segment - I didn’t attend that, but the next day when they taped the in-tent segments.

Fun things

Beat a Pokemon game

With the previously mentioned old DS Lite and Pokemon Platinum, I beat Spear Pillar sometime in August-ish. So now I just have to defeat the Elite Four. I should have more time at school to finish this.

Get a ridiculous selfie with a Silicon Valley icon

This goal was in the vein of “I’m in the Bay Area, let’s do some silly/stupid Silicon Valley things”. I did plenty other things in this vein (chasing Snap Spectacles to Big Sur, feeling like I was in a ridiculously tech-oriented area) that I didn’t feel any need for this one specifically.

Get a ride around an amazing racetrack

This one was within grasp for a lot of my co-op, but I didn’t think about it seriously much until the end. I bought a bone-stock BMW E30, which would be a great candidate for a track day (as long as it survived and I could drive it home), but I never got it to one. I did network/make friends with people who go to track days at Laguna Seca, Thunderhill, and Sonoma, so this can definitely still happen in the future.

Go to a SF Cars and Coffee and/or Pebble Beach Concours d’Elegance

Neither of these happened, for two different reasons. SF Cars and Coffee has been cancelled for most of the last eight months, and I couldn’t get away during the week of Pebble Beach for the hour-and-a-half (one way) drive. Almost all of the cheap activites are during the week. However, I’ve realized these events are less important; I didn’t have trouble meeting people who enjoyed cars. The Bay Area is teeming with them, including the person who I sold my E30 to.

Solve a Rubik’s Cube

I completely forgot about this one. It’s honestly not that important - something to try when I have free time and I’m looking for a puzzle like this.

Become OK at skateboarding

This was contingent on buying a helmet, which I never got around to. I did skate around for about half an hour with one not-great fall, in total. It was difficult finding places to skateboard where it’s socially acceptable to skateboard and feels like an open/welcoming space to newcomers. Also, it feels impossible to take good falls like it is for skiing or snowboarding.

Goals that I’m not sure how to tackle

Stop chewing my nails

This didn’t happen, either. I didn’t put much thought to it besides further recognizing the need to do so. I think I might try fidget tools and painting my nails next.

Have a better sense of style and wardrobe

I think I made progress on this front. My work attire started as a button-up with jeans or chinos, and gradually became hoodies with a more colorful set of V-necks and jeans or chinos. I also tried bleaching and then dying my hair, which I thought worked well and I should play around with more. I was definitely more conscientous.

I want to work on removing hoodies largely from my wardrobe - that really sticks out as being lazy, male, and tech. I also want to explore/mess around more with my hair.

Start cooking

This didn’t happen - there was cheap food for lunch and dinner that was far too accessible. I’m not sure if this’ll happen when I’m full-time either: I have too many things I want to do with my time, and far too many cool and interesting places to eat in the Bay Area in my free time. I should really prioritize this… ugh.

Help ship an iOS app

I ended up not working on non-work projects during my co-op. I would still like to do this, but I feel similarly to my involvement in FIRST Robotics and Chezy Champs: it’s simply not as interesting to me anymore, and I’d rather try other things.

Own a console/play more video games

I bought an Xbox One in September that I sold right before I finished my co-op. I rarely played it, despite having my two favorite games (Forza Motorsports 6 and Forza Horizon 2). I did end up playing some PC games (namely Team Fortress 2 - which essentially is like comfort food to me - and some Counter Strike: Global Offensive). I could see myself maybe playing more PC games (and building a desktop) to have a social thing to do with friends farther away. Console games have less appeal to me, other then for exclusives.

Write more

I’ve made some progress on this front. In October, I spent $50 on the macOS and iOS apps for Day One to serve as an impetus to start journaling. I’m successfully journaling every couple of days (usually every day, occassionally twice a week). That’s served as good writing practice
